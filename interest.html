My Interest in IT

My Interest in IT started when I was a child, our first computer was a Compaq Presario Desktop Computer running Windows 95, and my parents could not keep me away from it. From that point on I have always had an interest in computers and how they worked.

As I got older my fascination with computers continued, although as for most of my life I knew more about computers than my entire family, I had a false sense of knowledge and over time stopped learning new things.

In the IT world things change and evolve very quickly, so although I have stayed interested in IT and computers and kept up with things on a more casual basis, I am probably about a decade or more behind in my IT education.

As a Kid I remember messing around with HTML and C++, however I do not think I remember any of it. I remember discovering you could open a programs files in notepad and see and change the code, and that made me feel smart and I loved it.

Fast forward over 15 years later and this year I finally got around to building my first computer, something I had been planning to do since 2012. This made me reflect on my love for computers and lament the fact that I had not put any effort into studying it.

I chose to study IT at RMIT because I had heard good things from past students studying other subjects both on campus and online. Ultimately though it was the timing of the course starting that got me to enrol.

In this course I expect to catch up on the IT education I have missed out on over the last 2 decades and learn new and exciting things that can allow me to one day have a career in IT.

Whether that is as simple as fixing computers in a computer shop or as extravagant as working for Intel or Microsoft, I believe a career in IT would be a great fit for me.
